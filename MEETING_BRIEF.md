# Executive Meeting Brief

- **Key Developments:** Expansion of small business tooling, shift toward ROI-based metrics, and a concerted industry push for "Physical AI" (robotics).
- **Risks:** Cybersecurity vulnerabilities in model evaluation environments and the inherent difficulty of keeping "long-horizon" (autonomous) agents aligned with user goals.
- **Opportunities:** Utilizing the new OpenAI small business program for operational cost reduction; adopting the "AI Scorecard" to measure internal project viability.
- **Recommended Actions:** 
  1. Audit current AI spend against the "AI Scorecard" metrics provided by OpenAI.
  2. Increase investment in "AI governance" training to match the rise of long-horizon autonomous agents.
  3. Monitor "Physical AI" and robotics advancements for potential automation in manufacturing or logistics chains.

## Technology Trends

*   **From Chat to Agency:** The industry is moving from simple text generation to autonomous agents (like "Shippy") that can execute complex, multi-step workflows.
*   **Embodied AI:** Increased focus on "Physical AI"—integrating AI into robotics, supported by advanced simulation software.
*   **Governance & Accountability:** Organizations are moving away from raw "hype" toward professional metrics (ROI, cost-per-task) and increased board-level oversight.
*   **Security Transparency:** Leading AI firms are adopting a "proactive disclosure" model regarding security breaches to build institutional trust.

## Terminology

*   **Long-Horizon Models:** AI systems designed to work on complex tasks that take a long time to complete, requiring the model to maintain focus and context over many steps.
*   **Physical AI:** The integration of AI into physical hardware (like robots) to help them perceive, learn, and interact with the physical world.
*   **Return on Compute:** A metric measuring how much useful work is produced relative to the amount of computing power (and money) consumed.
*   **Model Evaluation:** The process of testing an AI model before it is released to ensure it is safe, accurate, and behaves as expected.
*   **Autonomous Agents:** AI software that can perform tasks on its own, such as using other software tools or navigating websites, without needing the user to give every single command.
